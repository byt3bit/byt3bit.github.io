Arnab Roy

Cybersecurity Research Group . D!ARC. Alpen-Adria-Universität Klagenfurt.

I am a lecturer at the Alpen-Adria Unviersity Klagenfurt, Austria.

I was a senior research associate at the Unviersity of Bristol, UK in the cryptography research group from 2017 to 2019. Prior to that I was postdoc researcher (2014-2017) at the Technical University of Denmark in the Cybersecurity group.

I received PhD in Computer Science from the University of Luxembourg in 2014. My doctoral research topic was security aspects of symmetric-key primitives and my thesis supervisor was Prof. Alex Biryukov.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in the area of cryptography and its applications. I am interested in designing cryptographic primitives, cryptanalysis, and application of cryptography in security and privacy. I am also interested in statistical machine learning and its interplay with cryptography and security.

My cryptographic designs

I am co-designer of the ZKP and MPC friendly symmetric-key families of primitives MiMC, GMiMC and most recently published Poseidon. I am co-designer of the lightweight encryption function ForkSkinny associated with the lightweight authenticated encryption scheme ForkAE. ForkAE is now one of the Round 2 candidates in the NIST competition for standardizing lightweight cryptography.

Professional service

I served as a PC member of Indocrypt 2020, PKC 2020, FSE (ToSC) 2019.
